Rehabilitation Department

Pain point & solution analysis

Pain Points Solutions
Therapy and rehabilitation costs are not always covered by health insurance making it difficult for some people to get access to comprehensive therapy Income based payment mode: It helps patients to pay for therapy or rehabilitation based on their income.
Muscle spasticity can interfere with ambulation, work or self-care in MS patients. Exoskeleton system for lower limb support: An exoskeleton wearable brace support with a computer-based control system, and motion sensors to aid in regaining strength.
Patients with MS can also suffer from psychological issues and need to adapt their surroundings to the existing abilities. Lifestyle coaching: Lifestyle coaching can help patients to accept and handle the challenges they face, e.g., muscle weakness, sensory disturbances, fatigue etc.
Sound therapy: Beyond the obvious calming affects, it also encourages body movements to a rhythmic beat
Some patients have problems to attend therapy sessions due to bad infrastructure or disease-related causes Tele-coaching: Tele-coaching helps patient to attend rehabilitation sessions anytime from everywhere, leading to more frequent sessions to handle the progression of MS better.
Due to the long disease course, patients eventually lose the motivation to continue rehabilitation. Game-based rehabilitation : 3D immersive games for an interactive and low-cost full body rehabilitation can induce motivation in patients with prolonged illness.
A lot of data is generated by different therapists (e.g., physiotherapist, cognitive therapist) and can be difficult to manage. Cloud data storage: By using cloud storage, data from different therapists can be easily transferred to patientâ€™s database.
MS patients benefit from at least 30 minutes of physical activity three days a week. However, due to potential progression of the disease it might be difficult to adhere to a consistent training schedule. Wearable: Motion wearable devices combined with smartphone applications allow telemonitoring and tele-coaching that aim to reinforce self-management, enhance physical activity levels, and improve fatigue. Patients thereby more successfully keep up a sufficient training regiment.
