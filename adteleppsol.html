Pain Points Solutions
Each stage of AD imposes different responsibilities on informal caregivers, increasing their burden. This leads to increased stress and decreased quality of life Tele-Medicine:While video conferencing cannot take the place of an in-person physical assessment, especially in rural areas contact to a health-care professional via video or telephone conference can support caregivers in difficult situations.
Patients who experience a stroke are at a higher risk of developing AD. This risk seems to increase by severity and frequency of the experienced stroke(s). Image-guided endovascular robotics: While already existing for cardiac catheterization, in future image-guided robotic-assisted therapies could also be an option for stroke care. This can potentially improve workflow and patient outcomes with safer and more precise treatments as well as an expanded access to care via telerobotics.
