Pain Points Solutions
It is still challenging to rule out all other possible causes before diagnosing AD, making new imaging technologies a necessity. 3T MRI Scanners: 3T MRI Scanners: Using powerful 3T MRI technology helps clinicians to assess atrophy and changes in tissue characteristics in AD patients. This allows the assessment of medial temporal atrophy which has been shown to have a predictive value for AD and is a proposed criteria for diagnosing AD at a pre-dementia stage.
Associated with the use of antibodies targeting amyloid-beta are amyloid-related imaging abnormalities. They include brain edema or hemosiderin deposits resulting from hemorrhage and need to be frequently checked via MRI, which is time-consuming. Turbo Suite technology: These software packages are equipped with unique parallel imaging, multi-slice, and compressed sensing technologies, enabling faster MRI scans. Turbo Suite Excelerate can significantly increase productivity with up to 50% exam time savings for all contrasts, orientations and body regions.
Deep Resolve Swift Brain: Deep Resolve is an AI-powered image reconstruction technology. By employing targeted denoising and convolutional neural networks, it generates high resolution images from low resolution input. With Deep Resolve Swift Brain neuro exams can be done in two minutes scan time making scans can faster and boosting workflow efficiency.
AI-Rad Companion Brain MR: This post-processing analysis software assists clinicians in viewing, analyzing and evaluating MRI brain images. It enhances the interpretation process via automatic segmentation and analysis of individual brain structures, the volumes can then be compared to a normative data.
